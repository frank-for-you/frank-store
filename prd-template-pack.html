What's included

  • 📋

    Feature PRD

    The workhorse. A full product requirement document for a single feature — problem statement, user stories, acceptance criteria, edge cases, metrics, and launch checklist. The kind you'd write for anything that takes more than a sprint to ship.

  • 🗺️

    Epic / Initiative Brief

    For larger bets. Frames the strategic problem, the proposed solution space, success metrics, and key risks. Designed to be readable by non-technical stakeholders without losing engineering clarity.

  • 🔬

    Technical Spike Template

    For exploration and research tasks. Defines the question being answered, the scope of investigation, how success is measured, and where the findings will be recorded.

  • 📝

    RFC Template

    Request for Comments — for decisions that need buy-in from multiple stakeholders. Structured to surface tradeoffs, not just present a conclusion. Forces you to write down the alternatives you considered and why you rejected them.

  • ⚡

    One-Pager

    When you need to move fast and don't have time for a full PRD. A tight, one-page structure that captures problem, proposed solution, and key constraints. Useful for quick decisions or async alignment.

  • 💡

    Filled Examples + Commentary

    Every template ships with a filled-in example and inline comments explaining what belongs in each section and why. Not just a skeleton — a guide.

FAQ

What format are the templates in?
Markdown (compatible with any editor, GitHub, Obsidian, etc.) and Notion-compatible formats. Both are included for every template. You pick whichever fits your workflow — or use both.
Is this for technical teams, product teams, or both?
Both. The templates are designed to be written by PMs or engineers and readable by either. The RFC and Technical Spike templates skew technical; the one-pager and Epic Brief work for any audience. The feature PRD sits in the middle.
When will it ship?
Targeting Q2 2026. Follow the blog for updates or email us to be notified.
Can I use these in my organization?
Yes. MIT licensed. Use them internally, adapt them, share them with your team. The only restriction is selling them as a standalone product.
Will there be more templates added over time?
Yes. Paying customers get access to all future additions. The initial pack will focus on the five core types; follow-up additions may include retrospective templates, design briefs, and QA test plans.
